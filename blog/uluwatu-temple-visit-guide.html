Uluwatu Temple: History, Tips and Best Time to Visit

April 03, 2026 · Uluwatu Paradise

The Rich History of Uluwatu Temple

Nestled atop towering limestone cliffs, Uluwatu Temple is one of Bali's most iconic spiritual sites. Known locally as Pura Luhur Uluwatu, this destination is revered not only for its stunning ocean views but also for its spiritual significance. Legend has it that the temple was established in the 11th century by a Javanese sage, making it one of the oldest religious sites on the island.

Architectural Significance

The temple's architecture is a testament to traditional Balinese design, with intricate stone carvings and a series of gates leading visitors through sacred spaces. It's no wonder that an Uluwatu Temple visit is a must-do for anyone traveling to Bali.

Tips for a Memorable Uluwatu Temple Visit

Maximize your experience by following these insider tips:

  • Dress Appropriately: As a place of worship, modest attire is required. Sarongs are available at the entrance if needed.
  • Visit at Sunset: The temple is renowned for its breathtaking sunsets. Arrive early to secure a good spot.
  • Beware of Monkeys: The temple is home to many playful monkeys known for snatching personal items. Keep your belongings secure.

Experiencing the Kecak Dance

One of the highlights of visiting Uluwatu is the traditional Kecak dance performed against the backdrop of a setting sun. This unique dance is a cultural spectacle of hypnotic chanting and dramatic storytelling, depicting scenes from the Ramayana. Booking your tickets in advance is advisable.

Best Time to Visit Uluwatu Temple

While the temple is open year-round, the best time for an Uluwatu Temple visit aligns with Bali's dry season, from April to October. During these months, you’ll enjoy clear skies and pleasant temperatures, perfect for exploring the temple grounds and surrounding areas.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the best time to visit Uluwatu Temple?

The best time to visit is during Bali's dry season, from April to October, for clear skies and pleasant weather.

Is there an entrance fee for Uluwatu Temple?

Yes, there is a small entrance fee that includes a sarong rental.

Can I watch the Kecak dance at Uluwatu Temple?

Yes, the Kecak dance is performed daily at sunset, and tickets can be purchased at the temple.
